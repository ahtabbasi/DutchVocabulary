# Dutch Verb Conjugation Practice - Regular Verbs (A2 Level)

Practice translating these English sentences to Dutch. Focus on regular verb conjugation in present tense, perfectum (past participle), and imperfectum (simple past). This exercise includes questions, negative statements, inversie, subordinate clauses, and modal verbs.

## Tips for Regular Verb Conjugation

- **Present tense:** Remove -en from the infinitive, add appropriate endings (-, -t, -en)
- **Perfectum:** Use "hebben" or "zijn" + past participle (ge- + verb stem + -d/-t)
- **Imperfectum:** Remove -en, add -de/-te endings based on the verb stem

## Notes on Sentence Structures

**Inversie (Inversion):** Occurs when the subject and verb switch places. This happens in:
- Yes/no questions (sentences 2, 5, 9, 13, 17)
- Question word questions (sentences 9, 13, 15)
- Sentences starting with time/place expressions (sentences 4, 7, 10, 16, 19)

**Main Clause + Subordinate Clause:** When a main clause is followed by a subordinate clause introduced by "dat" (that), "omdat" (because), "als" (if), etc. (sentences 1, 3, 6, 12, 15, 18)

**Subordinate Clause + Main Clause:** When a subordinate clause comes first, followed by a comma and then the main clause. This causes inversie in the main clause. (sentences 4, 7, 10, 16, 19, 20)

**Modal Verbs:** Dutch modal verbs include kunnen (can), moeten (must/should), willen (want), mogen (may), and zullen (will/shall). When used with another verb, the second verb stays in infinitive form. (sentences 2, 5, 8, 11, 14, 17, 20)
