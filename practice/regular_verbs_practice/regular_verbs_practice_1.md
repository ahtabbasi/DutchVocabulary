## Tips for Regular Verb Conjugation

- **Present tense:** Remove -en from the infinitive, add appropriate endings (-, -t, -en)
- **Perfectum:** Use "hebben" or "zijn" + past participle (ge- + verb stem + -d/-t)
- **Imperfectum:** Remove -en, add -de/-te endings based on the verb stem

## Notes on Inversie (Inversion)

**Inversie** occurs when the subject and verb switch places. This happens in:
- **Yes/no questions:** Verb comes first, then subject (e.g., "Studeer jij...?" instead of "Jij studeert...")
- **Question word questions:** Question word + verb + subject (e.g., "Waar koopt zij...?" instead of "Zij koopt waar...")
- **Sentences starting with time/place expressions:** The expression comes first, then verb + subject (e.g., "Elke ochtend drink ik..." instead of "Ik drink elke ochtend...")

**Examples in this exercise:** Sentences 2, 4, 5, 8, 9, 11, 12, 14, 15, 16, 17, 19, and 20 all demonstrate inversie.
